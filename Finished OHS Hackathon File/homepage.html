Overview

This course is a two-semester architecture course in the design and development of unique, structural buildings. The theme of the course, “Can we fix it? Yes we can!” focuses on solving structural issues and preventing further damage.

Course materials include a wrap around toolkit, a red and orange checkered shirt, blue overalls, and a yellow construction hat. Dr. Builder appreciates students who follow his instructions, especially on proper attire.

Writing Assignments
Bob is super chill and rarely gives out assignments. Therefore, in order to keep his students busy, he assigns them fun, ungraded essays, usually involving some form of pop culture. During sit down lessons, Bob watches movies and sitcoms along with his students in order to better prepare them for their essays.
In the first semester, students turn in three major assignments in addition to their course-related woodwork: the first asks students to further analyze the popular sitcom "Friends", the second looks at the success of Marvel comics, and the third investigates the complex characters presented in "The Office."

Course Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Binge watch sitcoms and movies effortlessly
  • Utilize filler words to fulfill essay requirements
  • Learn how to style overalls with construction hats
  • Fix basic construction issues
  • Chill with Dr. Builder
